About The Fulmars

In 2020, over 86% of 98 investigated fulmars were found with plastic inside their digestive system. Where over 40% contain over 0.1 gr of plastic, endangering their health. As the biomonitor of marine litter, the UK and EU targets to reduce to 10% of fulmars with more than 0.1 gram of plastic by 2074, as its acceptable threshold. As plastic production escalates, can they reach their target?

Furthermore, unable to differentiate plastic from food, fulmars tend to forage on the surface water and are likely to capture microplastic instead. As parents, the fulmar feed (or transfer) plastic to their fledglings. As the fledgling grew up and have a child of their own, it is likely that they will also feed plastic to their own fledglings. As in any living creatures, digesting plastic is not healthy. It can cause tearing inside their body, plastic chemicals absorbed into their body, and much more. Hence, shortening their lifespan.

Our Goal

And so, we would like to show you the brutal realities of fulmars and their future generations. Must their future generations suffer the same grim fate?

With Maru Mori, you can explore the changes of these 98 investigated fulmars on the occurrence of plastic found inside the fulmars that are illustrated through suspended lights.

How to interact?

First, take a look at the gear and explore.

Then, try and turn the outer gear which represents the years.

Finally, look up and see the light color changes!
